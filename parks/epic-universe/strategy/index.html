Which Ride to Rope Drop?

Universal expanded the official EPA list on Feb 14. Mine-Cart, Ministry, and the Dragon rides are now included. The catch: rope drop waits still swing wildly day-to-day.

The variability problem: Mine-Cart posted 25 min on Feb 12 and 90 min on Feb 13. Mario Kart: 15 min one day, 60 min the next. A static rope drop plan is a gamble.

3-day rope drop data (Feb 11-13):

  • Mine-Cart (now official EPA) opens ~8:06-8:11. Rope drop: 25-120 min depending on the day.
  • Mario Kart opens at EPA start (~8:00-8:16). Rope drop: 15-60 min.
  • Ministry opens before park open (~8:30-8:45). Rope drop: 120-150 min. Always high. Save for evening (~43 min by 9 PM).
  • Werewolf and Monsters are still walk-ons. 5-15 min. Don't waste your rope drop on these.

The Strategy: During EPA, hit Mario Kart first (15-60 min), then Mine-Cart. Save Ministry for 7-9 PM. But check live wait times before committing. If Mine-Cart posts 90+ at rope drop, consider Dragon Racer or Hiccup first instead.

Updated Feb 14: Mine-Cart, Ministry, Hiccup, and Dragon Racer are now officially on the EPA list. The rides dropped from EPA (Stardust, Carousel, Monsters, Werewolf) are short waits anyway.

No Early Entry? Here's Your Data-Driven Plan

No hotel package? No problem. We analyzed 722,000+ wait time samples to build the optimal strategy for guests entering at general admission. The key insight: don't chase the rides Early Entry guests already filled up.

The reality at opening: Early Entry guests have been riding for an hour. Mine-Cart is already at 98 min. Ministry is at 136 min. Mario Kart is at 76 min. Don't burn your golden hour on rides that are already slammed.

The strategy: Sprint the 3 rides that double in wait time within 2 hours. Fill the midday with rides that stay short all day. Push headliners to evening when waits drop 40-60%.

Arrival: Get There by 8 AM

Note: All times below assume a 9 AM opening. Some days open at 10 AM instead. Check the Universal app for your day and shift everything by an hour if needed.
  • Arrive by 8:00 AM. You'll need time for parking, security, and ticket scan.
  • Non-EE guests are let into Celestial Park (the central hub) before official opening, but you can't enter the world portals until general admission starts.
  • Position yourself near the Isle of Berk portal while you wait. That's where you're headed first.

Phase 1: Rope Drop Sprint (First 90 Min)

Only 3 rides punish you for waiting. Everything else stays manageable all day. These three double in wait time within 2 hours of opening.

9:05 AM
Hiccup's Wing Gliders. ~29 min wait at opening. Doubles to 86 min by 11 AM, the steepest penalty in the park. Walk straight through the Isle of Berk portal.
9:40 AM
Dragon Racer's Rally. ~30 min wait. Same land as Hiccup, walk right over. Hits 58 min by 11 AM.
10:15 AM
Curse of the Werewolf. ~20 min wait. Not on Early Entry, so it opens fresh for everyone at general admission. But hits 60 min by 11 AM. One portal over in Dark Universe.
Done in under 2 hours. Three time-sensitive rides banked. Total wait: ~79 minutes for 3 rides.

Phase 2: Ride When Short (All Day)

These rides never get painful. Slot them in whenever you see a short posted wait. Between meals, after shows, during your midday break.

Midday (11 AM-5 PM): Explore the incredible theming in every world. Catch shows (Untrainable Dragon in Isle of Berk, Le Cirque Arcanus in Wizarding World). Eat. Don't queue for headliners, they're at peak waits.

Phase 3: Evening Push (7 PM-Close)

This is when non-EE guests make up ground. Most rides drop 40-60% from their peak.

7:00 PM
Mario Kart: Bowser's Challenge. ~57 min wait (down from 114 at noon). Drops further to 39 at 8 PM and 27 min at 9 PM.
8:15 PM
Battle at the Ministry. ~77 min wait (down from 168 at 2 PM). Queue early enough, they may cut the line before close. Drops to 64 min by 9 PM.

Pro tip: If the park closes at 10 PM and Ministry posts 60+ min at 9 PM, you may not make it. Monitor the app. If Ministry drops below 70 min by 8 PM, jump in then.
