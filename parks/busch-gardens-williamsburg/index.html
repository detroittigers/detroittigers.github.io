Rides worth setting alerts for

Pantheon

Multi-launch coaster • pure intensity
180 ft73 mph4 launches2 inversions

World's fastest multi-launch coaster. Most intense ride in the park. Prone to downtime in wind, so alerts help you grab it when it's running.

Fun fact: Priority #1 every visit. When Pantheon is open, you go.

Griffon

Dive coaster • 90° drop
205 ft71 mphfloorless

Holds you at the top before the drop. Low throughput means long lines build fast. Track it and pounce on short windows.

Fun fact: That held moment at the edge is the whole ride, and it never gets old.

Apollo's Chariot

Hypercoaster • 9 airtime hills
210 ft73 mph9 hills

Smooth B&M classic with great re-rideability. Set alerts and chain multiple rides when it dips.

Fun fact: One of the most beloved B&M hypers ever built.

Alpengeist

Inverted coaster • 6 inversions
170 ft67 mph6 inversions

World's tallest complete-circuit inverted coaster. Hits hardest in the morning, so track it early.

Fun fact: Themed as a "ski lift gone rogue" through the Alps.

DarKoaster

Indoor straddle coaster • total darkness
4 launchesindoor

North America's first all-indoor straddle coaster (2023). Novelty drives demand and being indoor means it runs in all weather.

Fun fact: You ride "snowmobile" vehicles through total darkness.

Verbolten: Forbidden Turn

Launch coaster • indoor/outdoor
launchindoor/outdoor

Full thematic overhaul reopening Spring 2026 as "Black Forest Motor Tours." Expect high demand on reopen.

Fun fact: The indoor section features a surprise free-fall drop.

InvadR

Wooden coaster • BGW's only woodie
74 ft48 mph

BGW's only wooden coaster. Low capacity means lines build fast. Worth tracking for short windows.

Fun fact: A compact woodie that packs more punch than its size suggests.

If you only have one day

  1. Set alerts for Pantheon + Apollo's Chariot as primaries.
  2. Check Germany section timing. If open, hit DarKoaster and Verbolten first.
  3. Hit Pantheon early. It's prone to wind closures, so ride it when it's running.
  4. Use the Skyride to hop between park ends instead of walking.
  5. Save Griffon for late afternoon when the held-drop crowd thins out.

FAQ

Does Ride Ready use the park's official wait times?

Ride Ready tracks posted waits and trends so you can time rides better. If a park pauses updates or has downtime, your alerts may be delayed or temporarily less accurate.

What's the fastest way to get value on a one‑day visit?

Pick 4–6 headliners, favorite them, and set a few "drop" alerts. When something dips, you pivot. That's where you claw back time without buying a line‑skip add‑on.
