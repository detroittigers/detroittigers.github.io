Carowinds: Know When to Go

Track Carowinds wait times and set Ride Ready alerts for Fury 325, Copperhead Strike, Afterburn, Thunder Striker, Hurler and more — catch coaster windows without wasting your day in queues.

Quick park facts

LocationCharlotte, NC (straddles the NC/SC state line)
Best forWorld-class coasters — Fury 325 rated #1 steel coaster 10 consecutive years
What to trackFury 325, Copperhead Strike, Afterburn, Thunder Striker, Hurler
The trickFury 325 is right at the entrance — resist the urge to queue immediately. Check the app first; it can be shorter mid-afternoon.

Rides worth setting alerts for

Fury 325

Giga coaster • pure speed
325 ft95 mph6,602 ft long81° drop

This is the one people plan around. Alerts help you ride it without sacrificing half the day to standby.

Fun fact: #1 steel coaster in the world (Golden Ticket Awards 2016–2025). Crosses the NC/SC state line twice during the ride.

Copperhead Strike

Launch coaster (Mack) • double launch
2 launches5 inversions

Capacity-constrained launch coaster — alerts are essential to avoid long waits for a short ride window.

Fun fact: First launch coaster in the Carolinas. Blue Ridge moonshine runner theme throughout the queue.

Afterburn

Inverted coaster (B&M) • 6 inversions
6 inversionsB&M invert

Surprisingly popular — catch the dips between rushes. One of the best B&M inverts in the country.

Fun fact: Unusually smooth for a 1999 coaster. Still considered one of the top inverted coasters anywhere.

Thunder Striker

Hypercoaster (B&M) • airtime machine
232 ft75 mph7 airtime hills

High capacity B&M keeps lines moving, but it still stacks up on busy days. Track it for a quick win.

Fun fact: Formerly Intimidator (Dale Earnhardt license expired). Seven major airtime hills make it a floater paradise.

Hurler

Wooden coaster • classic out-and-back
WoodenOut-and-back

A perfect 'backup alert' coaster — decent lines on busy days, and a quick win when it dips.

Fun fact: Received re-tracking in 2024, giving it a smoother ride than it's had in years.

If you only have one day

  1. Set alerts for Fury 325 and Copperhead Strike as your primaries.
  2. Hit Copperhead Strike first at rope drop (back of park, lower initial crowd).
  3. Afterburn mid-morning while Fury builds its line.
  4. Save Fury 325 for late afternoon or closing when lines drop.
  5. Use Carolina Harbor waterpark as your midday heat break (included with admission).

FAQ

Does Ride Ready use the park's official wait times?

Ride Ready tracks posted waits and trends so you can time rides better. If a park pauses updates or has downtime, your alerts may be delayed or temporarily less accurate.

What's the fastest way to get value on a one-day visit?

Pick 4-6 headliners, favorite them, and set a few "drop" alerts. When something dips, you pivot — that's where you claw back time without buying a line-skip add-on.

No. Ride Ready is an independent app.
