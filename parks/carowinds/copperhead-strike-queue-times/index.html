Tips for Copperhead Strike

  • Most volatile wait times in the park. Copperhead Strike swings from 14 minutes to 40 minutes throughout the day. No other Carowinds ride is this unpredictable.
  • Hit it early (10-11 AM). The morning window is your safest bet at 14-15 minutes. After that, it's a rollercoaster of wait times (pun intended).
  • Capacity-constrained launch coaster. Launch coasters inherently have lower throughput than traditional lift-hill coasters, which is why waits spike so sharply with demand.
  • The 5 PM dinner dip is real. If you missed the morning, 5 PM (22 min) offers a brief reprieve before evening waits climb again.
  • Blue Ridge moonshine runner theme is excellent. The queue theming and storyline are worth paying attention to — it's one of the best-themed coasters at Carowinds.

FAQ

When should I ride Copperhead Strike?

Between 10-11 AM when average waits are 14-15 minutes. This is a capacity-constrained launch coaster with the most volatile wait times in the park — swinging from 14 minutes to 40 minutes. Don't wait until afternoon; the 4 PM peak hits 40 minutes average.

How many launches does Copperhead Strike have?

Copperhead Strike has 2 launches. It's a Mack Rides launch coaster that accelerates you through two separate launch sections, combined with 5 inversions throughout the layout. The dual-launch design gives it a unique pacing compared to traditional lift-hill coasters.

Is Copperhead Strike rough?

No. Copperhead Strike is a Mack Rides coaster, and Mack is known for smooth ride experiences. The lap bar restraints are comfortable, and the transitions between inversions and launches are well-profiled. It's an intense ride but not a rough one.

What is the height requirement for Copperhead Strike?

Copperhead Strike has a 52-inch (132 cm) height requirement. Riders must be at least 52 inches tall to ride.
