Tips for Afterburn

FAQ

Is Afterburn one of the best inverted coasters?

Yes. Afterburn is widely regarded as one of the best B&M inverted coasters in the country. Despite opening in 1999, it consistently ranks among the top inverted coasters in enthusiast polls. Its compact, intense layout with 6 inversions and sustained forces throughout make it a standout in the B&M invert lineup.

When is Afterburn shortest?

At 10 AM, averaging 12 minutes. Morning is your best window. Avoid 8 PM, which spikes to 39 minutes due to a closing rush — similar to Fury 325's evening pattern.

How does Afterburn compare to other B&M inverts?

Afterburn holds its own against newer B&M inverts like Banshee and Montu. It's unusually smooth for a 1999 coaster, with a compact layout that maintains intensity throughout all 6 inversions. Many enthusiasts rank it in the top 3-5 B&M inverts ever built. It lacks the length of some newer models but makes up for it with relentless pacing.

What is the height requirement for Afterburn?

Afterburn has a 54-inch (137 cm) height requirement. Riders must be at least 54 inches tall to ride.
