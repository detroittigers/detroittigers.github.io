Tips for Alpen Fury

  • Don't rope drop. The 10-11 AM window is the worst hour because every visitor knows Alpen Fury is the new ride and tries to hit it first. The line forms before gate drop.
  • Use the morning on Behemoth, Yukon Striker, and Mighty Canadian Minebuster instead, three majors at sub-20 waits in 90 minutes.
  • Commit at 9:30 PM. Park-close line cutoff means you'll ride and the line behind you can't keep growing. The sunset Wonder Mountain launch is genuinely better at night with the IMAscore audio.
  • If you only have one day and Alpen Fury is the priority, Fast Lane Plus is worth it. This is the one ride at the park where it changes your day.
  • The vertical launch shaft drills straight through Wonder Mountain itself, you can hear the second launch fire from inside the queue.

FAQ

Should I rope drop Alpen Fury?

No. Alpen Fury opens at a 135-minute median wait, the worst hour of the day. Every visitor knows it's the new ride and tries to hit it first, so the line forms before gate drop and slowly clears through the day. The lowest median we observe is 90 minutes at park close. Spend the morning on Behemoth, Yukon Striker, and Mighty Canadian Minebuster instead.

When does Alpen Fury have the shortest wait?

10 PM, the closing hour. Median wait drops to 90 minutes. The 6-9 PM window also runs in the 100-110 range. Park-close line cutoff means committing at 9:30 PM is optimal: you ride and the line behind you can't grow further.

How many inversions does Alpen Fury have?

Nine. That's the world record for any launched roller coaster. Alpen Fury features a vertical launch shaft drilled through Wonder Mountain itself, dual launches that kick from 43 mph to 71.5 mph in two seconds, and a synchronized IMAscore audio track timed to both launches and a splash-down mist effect.

What replaced when Alpen Fury was built?

Xtreme SkyFlyer, the park's tower swing attraction. Alpen Fury opened July 12, 2025 as the first new Canada's Wonderland coaster of the Six Flags-Cedar Fair merger era and is built into Alpenfest, the German-themed area on the back side of Wonder Mountain.
